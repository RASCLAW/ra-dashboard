Claude Dispatch: Run Agents From Your Phone

March 24, 2026 Source: YouTube + Web Researched by Claude
What Happened

Anthropic shipped Dispatch -- a feature in Claude Cowork that lets you assign tasks to Claude from your iPhone. Claude runs the task on your Mac while you're away, and you come back to completed work.

It creates one persistent conversation thread across phone and desktop. Paired with the new Computer Use feature (macOS research preview), Claude can now operate your desktop apps -- clicking, typing, navigating -- autonomously.

Available for Claude Pro and Max subscribers on macOS.

Unlike OpenClaw which requires self-hosting and technical setup, Dispatch works out of the box for Pro/Max subscribers. Nick Saraev's review highlights the security advantage -- your data stays on Anthropic's infrastructure rather than a third-party server.

How This Affects You

You're already running Claude Code at work via VSCode tunnel (dubery-dev). Dispatch could simplify this -- instead of maintaining a tunnel, you'd just text Claude from your phone and it works on your home Mac.

But there's a catch: you're on WSL2/Windows, not macOS. Computer Use is Mac-only for now. For you right now: keep the tunnel workflow. But watch for Windows support -- when it drops, it replaces your entire remote setup.

What You Can Do
  • Keep using the tunnel workflow for now (Windows/WSL2)
  • Watch for Windows Computer Use announcement
  • Test Dispatch on Arabelle's phone if she has Claude app -- even without Computer Use, the persistent conversation is useful
